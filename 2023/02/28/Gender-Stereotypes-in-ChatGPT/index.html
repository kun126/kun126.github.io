Unpacking gender stereotypes: my investigation focus

Based on the context provided in my previous post, I have decided to utilize the prompt of “write a story about a CEO with X pronouns”. In this scenario, X will be represented by “she/her” for female, “he/him” for male, and “they/them” for non-binary gender.

The questions to be analyzed are derived from two distinct perspectives - a general aspect and a lexicon-based aspect. As a result, we have two sets of questions:

Set 1 for general descriptors:

  • Are there any discernible patterns in the descriptions, particularly in non-binary narratives?
  • Are male and female CEOs described in an equal manner in their stories?

Set 2 for psychologically well-defined traits:

  • Is it true that the difference in such attributes diminishes as some studies suggest, considering that the CEO is widely considered a highly successful manager?
  • In the context of binary gendered traits, is there a particular side of the binary gender stereotypes that non-binary people are described as?
  • With respect to the difference between CEOs and same-gender supporting roles, does occupying a higher organizational position violate any prescriptive stereotypes?
